## Overview

Greys (or Grey Aliens) are the dominant entity-type in late-20th-century western UFO and 'alien abduction' lore. Witnesses describe them as short (typically 3–4 feet, occasionally up to 6 feet), thin humanoids with smooth grey skin, hairless oversized heads, large black almond-shaped eyes, vestigial or absent noses, ears, and mouths, and slim limbs with three or four long fingers. They are usually reported in 'medical examination' abduction scenarios with telepathic communication, emotionless behaviour, and (especially in Budd Hopkins's and David Jacobs's writings) reproductive procedures. The Grey image consolidated through the September 1961 Betty and Barney Hill abduction case in New Hampshire — first widely reported in 1965 — and exploded into mass culture with Whitley Strieber's bestseller Communion (1987), whose Ted Seth Jacobs cover painting became iconic. Sceptics including Robert Sheaffer, Martin Kottmeyer, Carl Sagan, and Susan Clancy have argued that the Grey is a culturally-transmitted image with documentable antecedents in H. G. Wells (1893) and the Swedish novelist Gustav Sandgren (1933), and that Barney Hill's 1964 hypnotic descriptions were specifically influenced by the Outer Limits episode 'The Bellero Shield' (broadcast 10 February 1964, twelve days before the first hypnosis session).

## Notable Sightings

### Betty and Barney Hill abduction ⭐
> **Date**: 1961-09-19  
> **Location**: US Route 3 between Lancaster and Concord, New Hampshire  
> **Witnesses**: 2 (Betty Hill (social worker), Barney Hill (postal worker))  
> **Evidence**: Worn dress and shoes (Betty Hill); Stopped wristwatches; Marks on car  
Driving south at night, the Hills observed a bright moving light near the Moon that descended towards them. Barney stopped the car, observed humanoid figures through binoculars, and panicked. Both reported a buzzing sound, a period of altered awareness, and — under hypnosis with Dr. Benjamin Simon in 1964 — described a medical examination aboard a disc-shaped craft by short, grey, large-eyed humanoids. John G. Fuller's 1966 book The Interrupted Journey brought the case to mass attention; researcher Marjorie Fish later interpreted Betty's recalled 'star map' as a depiction of Zeta Reticuli.
**Credibility**: medium — Foundational case of Grey-alien iconography. Sceptics cite the 'Bellero Shield' coincidence and known unreliability of hypnotic memory.
**Sources**: Fuller, J. G. (1966), The Interrupted Journey

## Theories & Explanations

### Paranormal / Cryptozoological

**Extraterrestrial visitation** _(Budd Hopkins, David M. Jacobs, John E. Mack)_

Greys are biological extraterrestrials visiting Earth, often associated with a hybridisation / breeding agenda in the Hopkins–Jacobs–Mack tradition.

**Interdimensional / non-physical entity** _(Jacques Vallée)_

Greys are non-physical or interdimensional entities that present themselves through the cultural framework available to the witness.

### Skeptical

**Cultural transmission and media influence** _(Robert Sheaffer, Martin Kottmeyer, Carl Sagan)_

Grey-alien imagery is a cultural product, traceable from H. G. Wells (1893) through Sandgren (1933), the Outer Limits 'Bellero Shield' (1964), and the Communion cover (1987). Witnesses describe what their culture expects.

**Sleep paralysis and false memory** _(Susan Clancy, Richard McNally)_

Many bedroom 'abduction' encounters fit the classical phenomenology of sleep paralysis with hypnopompic hallucinations; hypnotic 'recovery' of detail introduces confabulation.

**No physical evidence** _(Carl Sagan, Robert Sheaffer)_

After more than half a century, no specimen, body, or unambiguous craft has been delivered to mainstream science.
